# Czech Republic Legal Setup Guide

## Entity: s.r.o. (Společnost s ručením omezeným)

- Minimum capital: CZK 1 (effectively)
- Registration: [justice.cz](https://justice.cz) online portal (1–2 weeks)
- 100% foreign ownership permitted
- EU passport for all EU/EEA licences once registered

## Licence Matrix

| Service | Regulator | Licence | Timeline | Min Capital |
|---------|-----------|---------|----------|-------------|
| Forensic AI advisory | Živnostenský úřad | General trade | 1 week | None |
| Payment Institution | CNB | PSD2 PI (EU passport) | 4–6 months | EUR 20–125k |
| E-Money Institution | CNB | EMD2 EMI | 4–6 months | EUR 350k |
| Investment Advice | CNB | MiFID II Investment Firm | 6–12 months | EUR 125k |
| Crypto / VASP | CNB | MiCA CASP | 3–6 months | EUR 150k |

## AML Requirements (Czech Act 253/2008 + 6AMLD)

- KYC for all clients (identity + beneficial ownership)
- Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD) for transactions ≥ EUR 15,000
- Internal AML risk assessment and transaction monitoring
- Report suspicious transactions to FAÚ (Financial Analytical Office)
- PEP screening + FATF high-risk jurisdiction screening
- AML compliance officer appointment

## Recommended First Steps

1. Register s.r.o. at [justice.cz](https://justice.cz)
2. Obtain general trade licence (živnostenský úřad) for advisory AI
3. Open business bank account (prepare business plan)
4. Register with Ú00Ú if processing sensitive personal data at scale
5. Engage Czech fintech law firm for CNB licence application
6. Appoint AML compliance officer
7. Establish GDPR records of processing activities (Art. 30 GDPR)
